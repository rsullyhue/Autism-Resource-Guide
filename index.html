ğŸš€ What to Do First

I suspect a student might be autistic. What should I do?

Step 1: Document observations.
Step 2: Share concerns with parents supportively.
Step 3: Refer to Student Study Team.
Step 4: Initiate evaluation if appropriate.
Step 5: Connect family with Regional Center resources.

How do I request a special education evaluation?

Work with your school's special education coordinator. Parents must provide written consent. The evaluation must be completed within 60 days in California.

What is an IEP?

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legally binding document outlining specialized instruction, services, accommodations, and goals for a student with a disability.

ğŸ’¡ Key Communication Principles

Presume competence: All students have something to communicate.
Model AAC: Use the student's communication system yourself.
Honor all communication: Gestures, sounds, behaviors are all communication.
Give time: Processing can take longer - wait patiently.
Reduce pressure: Don't force eye contact or speech.

ğŸ”‘ Understanding Behavior in Autism

All behavior is communication. Challenging behaviors often communicate: "This is too hard," "I'm overwhelmed," "I don't understand," "I need a break," or "Something hurts."

Common functions: Escape/Avoid, Attention, Sensory, Tangible.

Prevention is key: Environmental modifications, visual supports, predictable routines, and teaching replacement skills prevent most challenging behaviors.

ğŸ”‘ Key Trauma-Informed Principles

Remember: Safety, trustworthiness, peer support, collaboration, empowerment, and cultural sensitivity are core principles. For autistic students, also consider sensory needs, communication differences, and the importance of predictability and routine. Trauma responses in autistic individuals may look different - meltdowns, shutdowns, or increased rigidity can all be trauma responses.
